Four years ago, I began furiously coding up ‘hello-world’s like a possum on sweet potato. Not long after, potatoless, I was diving headlong into neural nets with Coursera’s Andrew Ng.

The allure of grandiosity is a powerful one, but it’s also a vice. The real art is in not losing sight of simple joys — the ones that exist in coding a line that works, in crafting a sentence that sings, in savoring a moment of pure living.

This is what ehrror represents. A digital playground by Justin Ehringhaus for both the careful and the carefree exploration of ideas.

Path Recounted:

  • Portland, Maine raised. Place I call home.
  • Harbin, China study abroad. Palate grew beyond mac-and-cheese.
  • Kumamoto, Japan study abroad. Embarrassing foray into vlogging.
  • Bowdoin College bachelor’s. Jack of all trades, master of none.
  • Hiroshima, Japan study abroad. Best year of my youth.
  • Yamanashi, Japan work. Great perks. Pondering why I left.
  • New York City work. The hustle. Not finance.
  • Portland, Maine master’s. Data.
  • A fuller, more carefully constructed version: Resume.

What’s next? Here’s to the unknown and the joy of exploring it — one code, one word, one moment at a time.
